INTERNATIONAL ECONOMIC REVIEW

Macroeconomic Impacts of Digital Assets on Emerging Markets

Volume 44 • Issue 2 • February 2025
Abstract: This paper examines the correlation between high-frequency digital asset trading and the stability of local fiat currencies in developing economies. Using a structural vector autoregression (SVAR) model, we analyze data from 2020 to 2024 to determine whether digital liquidity provides a hedge against inflation or exacerbates capital flight.

1. Introduction

The rapid proliferation of decentralized financial protocols has introduced a new dimension to global capital flows. Traditionally, capital flight was restricted by institutional barriers and geographic boundaries. However, the advent of borderless digital ledgers has significantly reduced transaction costs and latency.

Our study focuses on the "Liquidity Paradox," where increased access to global digital markets can simultaneously stabilize household purchasing power while destabilizing national central bank reserves. We argue that the net effect is contingent upon the regulatory framework of the host nation.

2. Methodology

We utilized a dataset comprising daily price movements, on-chain transaction volumes, and macroeconomic indicators from 15 emerging markets. The model accounts for seasonal variations and global geopolitical shocks, including supply chain disruptions and interest rate adjustments by the Federal Reserve.

The core of our analysis rests on the Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE) technique to identify the most significant predictors of market volatility in the digital sector.
